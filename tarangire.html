📅 Best Time to Visit Tarangire - Month by Month

Tarangire changes completely between dry + wet season. Here’s what to expect:

Jan - Mar: Green Season, Baby Animals 🌿
Weather: Warm, short rains. Lush green landscape.
Animals: Elephants spread out. Many leave park. Harder to see big herds. BUT: Baby elephants, zebras, wildebeest everywhere.
Crowds: Low. Great for birding - 550+ species.
Price: Low season - save $40/day.
Apr - May: Heavy Rains, Avoid 🚫
Weather: Heavy daily rains. Some roads impassable.
Animals: Scattered. Tall grass hides everything. Many elephants migrate out.
Crowds: Almost empty.
Price: Cheapest, but not worth it. We don’t recommend.
Jun - Oct: DRY SEASON, ELEPHANT HEAVEN 🐘
Weather: Dry, cool, 15-27°C. Dusty but perfect game drives.
Animals: PEAK. 3000+ elephants crowd Tarangire River. See 300 in one herd. Lions, leopards hunt at river. Best time on earth for elephants.
Crowds: High. Book 3 months early.
Price: High season rates.
Nov - Dec: Short Rains, Transition 🌦️
Weather: Afternoon showers. Landscape turns green fast.
Animals: Elephants start dispersing. Still good but not peak. Great for photos - green + elephants.
Crowds: Medium. Good value.
Price: Shoulder season - save $25/day.

Why Tarangire?

Tarangire is only 2 hours from Arusha but feels like true wilderness. During dry season June to October, the Tarangire River is the only water source for miles. Thousands of elephants, zebras, and wildebeest come here to drink. You will see herds of 300+ elephants in one sighting.

3000+ Elephants

Largest elephant population in Tanzania. See massive herds up close every single day.

Baobab Trees

Ancient baobabs over 1000 years old. Iconic African landscapes perfect for photos.

Big Cats

Lions, leopards, and cheetahs. Lions here climb trees just like Lake Manyara.

No Crowds

Less tourist traffic than Serengeti. More exclusive safari experience.

Questions Every Smart Traveler Asks 🤔

Is Tarangire better than Serengeti for elephants?

Yes. Tarangire has 3000+ elephants vs 2000 in Serengeti. During dry season they all gather at the river. You'll see herds of 300+ at once. Serengeti is better for migration.

Why so few tourists if it's so good?

Most agents push Serengeti because it's famous. Tarangire is our secret. No crowds = better photos, more time with animals, cheaper prices.

Can we climb the baobab trees?

No climbing - they're 1000+ years old and protected. But we stop at the biggest ones for photos. Some trunks are 30m wide!

What to Expect on Your Tarangire Tour
